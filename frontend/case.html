You Are Not Alone

When a loved one goes missing, one of the hardest emotions families often experience is loneliness.

It can feel impossible to understand how something like this could happen, or whether anyone else truly understands what your family is going through.

This project was created to help show that disappearances are not always isolated incidents. In many cases, there are other individuals whose circumstances of disappearance are extremely similar.

The purpose of this website is not to investigate cases, draw conclusions, or replace law enforcement. Its purpose is to help families realize they may not be alone in the way their loved one disappeared.

Grief can already feel isolating, but grieving a disappearance that happened under unusual or deeply specific circumstances can feel even more impossible to explain to others. There is a difference between speaking with someone who has experienced loss, and speaking with someone whose loved one vanished in a strikingly similar way. This project was created in the hope that families may find understanding, connection, and community through shared circumstances that are often difficult to put into words.

If you feel this case is emotionally similar to the disappearance of your loved one and would like to possibly connect with the family involved, we encourage you to reach out to the investigating agency handling this case or contact NamUs directly for additional guidance.
